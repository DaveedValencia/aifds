Why Schema-First

AI crawlers still scrape HTML and marketing copy, producing hallucinations and eroding brand control. Canonical JSON reverses that drift by giving models structured, testable facts.

What AIFDS Provides

  • Opinionated JSON schema families built on schema.org best practices.
  • Validation guidance for publishing, versioning, and sharing machine-readable payloads.
  • Examples tuned for Shopify storefronts and service operators shipping trusted data.

Commerce Track (Shopify-first)

We are distilling the core surfaces that power ecommerce discovery — from PDP accuracy to merchandising experiences — into portable, validated JSON schemas.

Artifacts in Draft

  • product.json — canonical SKU facts, availability, and merchandising tags.
  • homepage.json — hero messaging, featured collections, and promotional states.
  • collections.json — thematic groupings with filters, sort orders, and SEO handles.
  • blog.json — editorial summaries, canonical URLs, and cross-linking metadata.

Service Track

Service operators need a shared language for offers, delivery proof, and ROI. The services track captures how agencies and studios communicate their value to AI surfaces.

Draft Artifacts

  • service-homepage.json — positioning, proof points, testimonials, and CTAs.
  • services.json — catalog of offerings, deliverables, pricing models, and SLAs.
  • contact.json — intake workflows, preferred channels, and response windows.
  • pricing.json — packaged rates, prerequisites, and discount logic.

What’s Next

  • Publish reference payloads and validation checklists for each schema family.
  • Extend coverage to subscriptions, marketplaces, and post-purchase experiences.
  • Codify change management so partners can diff and verify updates in real time.

Get Involved

Contribute feedback, propose schema additions, or pilot the drafts with your storefront or studio. Reach out on GitHub or X — this standard grows with practitioners.
